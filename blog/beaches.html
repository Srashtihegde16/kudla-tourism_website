Top beaches around Mangalore

Mangalore is a beautiful coastal city located in the southern state of Karnataka in India. It has several scenic beaches that attract both locals and tourists.

Explore

1. Sasihitlu beach

n the northern part of Mangalore situated approximately 6 Km to the west of NH 66, is called Sasihitlu Beach. According to the tourists, this beach is among the refreshing and enjoyable places to visit in Mangalore. It is surrounded by the backwaters of two rivers, Pavanje and Shambhavi Rivers. Sasihitlu Beach is the place where these two rivers, Pavanje and Shambhavi meet. This unique thing about this beach gives it a special place in the Mangalore tour package. The white sand of the beach adds to its beauty.

2. Panambur beach

Panambur Beach is one of the most popular beaches in Mangaluru city in coastal Karnataka. Known to be one of the safest and well-maintained beaches, it is also famous for its dramatic sunset. The beach aura warms up during the carnivals, organized by the district authorities. The festivities include boat races, beach sports and sand sculpture contests. The beach has jet ski rides, boating, dolphin viewing, food stalls, besides trained beach lifeguards and patrol vehicles to ensure the safety of the visitors to the beach.

3. Tannerbhavi beach

Tannirbhavi beach is a beach in Mangalore, Karnataka, India. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in coastal Karnataka. Along with the beach, Sultan Battery, Tannirbavi Tree Park & the proposed Marine museum are also the tourist attractions. It can be reached either by land near Kuloor Bridge or by ferry via Gurupura river from Sultan Battery

4. NITK beach

NITK Beach is a popular beach located in the coastal town of Surathkal near Mangalore, in the state of Karnataka, India. It is also known as the Surathkal Beach or the Mukka Beach. The beach is situated at a distance of about 22 km from Mangalore and is easily accessible by road.

5. Someshwara beach

Someshwara Beach is a pristine and secluded beach located in Ullal, near Mangalore in the state of Karnataka, India. It is situated at a distance of about 9 km from Mangalore and is easily accessible by road. Someshwara Beach is known for its serene and untouched beauty, with clean white sand, clear blue waters and beautiful rock formations.The beach is also popular for its Shiva temple, which is located right on the beach. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and attracts many visitors who come to offer prayers and seek blessings. The beach is named after this temple.

6. Padubidri beach

Padubidri beach is more fun and vibrant with all the adventurous activities it has to offer to travelers. This beach is a kilometer away from the town of Padubidri. The beach is sandy and quite long. The beach has plenty of palm trees all over. The main portion of this beach has been paved with interlock tiles and plenty of park benches have been installed. People can relax and enjoy the perfect view of the sea. As the sunset approaches one can see the lights flashing from the lighthouses at Suratkal and Kaup which is a beautiful sight to behold. 

7. Kaup beach

Kapu is a beach village in coastal Karnataka. Kapu’s long sandy beaches offer a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea. With its tropical climate and a bevy of attractions that beckon tourists from across the country, Kapu is predominantly known for its greenery that surrounds the beach. One of the main attractions of the Kapu beach is the century-old 130 ft. lighthouse.

8. Malpe beach

Malpe beach is more fun and vibrant with all the adventurous activities it has to offer to travelers. This beach is a kilometer away from the town of Padubidri. The beach is sandy and quite long. The beach has plenty of palm trees all over. The main portion of this beach has been paved with interlock tiles and plenty of park benches have been installed.
