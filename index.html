About

Mangalore is a coastal city in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is the largest city in the Dakshina Kannada district and a major port city on the Arabian Sea coast. Mangalore is known for its unique blend of natural beauty and modern development, with a mix of sandy beaches, rolling hills, and bustling markets. The city is also famous for its rich cultural heritage and diverse communities, including the Tuluvas, Konkanis, and Bearys.  Mangalore is a hub for the petroleum, chemical, and banking industries, and is also a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over India and beyond. Some popular tourist attractions in Mangalore include the Tannirbhavi Beach, Kadri Hill Park, Mangaladevi Temple, and the Milagres Church.

Things To Do

Recommended
Trekking Mangalore is a popular destination for trekking enthusiasts. There are several trekking trails in and around Mangalore that offer stunning views of the Western Ghats and the lush greenery of the region.
Water Sports Mangalore, located on the coast of the Arabian Sea, is a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts. There are several water sports activities that you can enjoy in Mangalore, including Jet skiing,parasailing,Scuba Diving,Kayaking.
Food Mangalore is known for its unique and delicious cuisine, which is a blend of South Indian and Konkani flavors.
Beaches Mangalore is home to several beautiful beaches that attract tourists from all over the world. Some of the popular beaches of Mangalore are Panambur Beach,Tannirbavi Beach,Someshwara Beach,Surathkal Beach.
Temples Mangalore is home to several ancient and historic temples, each with its unique architecture and cultural significance.
Experience Culture Mangalore is a culturally rich city that boasts a blend of various cultures and traditions. The city has a vibrant history that dates back to several centuries and has been influenced by various dynasties, including the Kadambas, Vijayanagara Empire, and the British.

Experience Mangalore

kite festivalThe kite festival in Mangalore is a vibrant and colorful event that takes place during the harvest season. The festival involves flying kites of different shapes,sizes and colors. The kite festival is an important cultural event in Mangalore, with people coming together to celebrate.
CuisineMangalorean cuisine is a fusion of South Indian and coastal Karnataka cuisine. The cuisine is known for its use of aromatic spices, coconut-based curries, and fresh seafood. Popular dishes include fish curry, chicken ghee roast, neer dosa, and biryani.
Village LifeThe villages surrounding Mangalore are known for their lush greenery,paddy fields and coconut groves. The villagers often engage in occupations like fishing,agriculture,pottery and maintain their cultural traditions through folk music, dance and festivals.
FestivalsMangalore celebrates a variety of festivals throughout the year that reflect its diverse cultural traditions. Some festivals in Mangalore include Dasara and Bhoota kola. Other festivals include Diwali,Christmas,Eid, reflecting the city's multicultural and multi-religious fabric.
ArtformsMangalore has a rich cultural heritage, with a variety of art forms that reflect its diverse influences. Yakshagana, a traditional dance-drama, is one of the most popular art forms in Mangalore, known for its colorful costumes, dynamic music, and vibrant performances.
FishingMangalore is renowned for its traditional fishing methods, such as the use of Chinese fishing nets and the country boat. Fishing in Mangalore is not just a means of livelihood but also a part of the city's rich cultural heritage, making it a fascinating experience for visitors.
